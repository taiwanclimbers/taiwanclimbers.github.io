Welcome

Rock climbing is more than just a sport. It is a way for us to overcome fears,
strengthen our bodies and minds, discover spectacular places around the world, and interact with human beings from all walks of life. It can be humbling when discover your initial limitations, but also so much more empowering when you discover how you and your body are capable of overcoming them. All these aspects combine to create a pursuit that is addicting and often life changing.

On the north coast of Taiwan is Long Dong, one of the world's most spectacular sandstone sea-cliff climbing areas. There are hundreds of routes for all skill and interest levels, ranging from short routes of pure movement to full-on one day adventures. The area is about 90 minutes from Taipei by bus and has several small shops selling snacks, water, and beer.

"Climbing is a game in which we play at learning the skills necessary to a beautiful life" -Royal Robbins, Yosemite climbing legend.

Whether you've traveled around the world in search of beautiful rock or never before worn climbing shoes, our programs will inspire a connection with a magical and powerful art form in a truly awesome part of the world.

Climbing Packages

For Beginning Climbers

Indoor Package: An overview of what climbing is all about. You will learn to tie basic knots, general safety, and elementary technique. This set of courses is ideal for anyone eager to learn about climbing, but cannot commit an entire day to climbing outdoors.

Courses included: Introduction to Climbing (Artificial Wall), Movement Practice (Artificial Wall), Lead Climbing (Artificial Wall).

Day Trip to Long Dong: Meet at Taipei Main station's West bus terminal in the morning. Students can choose from a range of courses ranging from beginner to advanced. Healthy snacks are included, afternoon swimming is optional, and we will return to Taipei in the evening.

Courses included: Introduction to Climbing (Natural Rock), Movement Practice (Natural Rock).

Weekend Trip to Long Dong: Same as above, with an overnight stay to maximize climbing and enjoyment. We will camp in one of LD's namesake caves, with a simple dinner and breakfast included. Staying in the local hostel is also an option, however, at your own expense (about 600 NT).

Courses included: Introduction to Climbing (Natural Rock), Movement Practice (Natural Rock), Simple Anchors and Rappelling, Lead Climbing (Natural Rock), Lead Climbing Practice.

Our packages are designed for you to become as self-sufficient as soon as possible, so you can get on with what you really want to do: climb! We are, however, committed to your safety and comfort, so we provide extra care and attention as you require it.

For Experienced Climbers

Traditional Climbing Bonanza- The large sandstone blocks that make up Long Dong lend themselves perfectly for traditional climbing. Almost all of the climbs can be done by placing protection instead of using the drilled bolts. We offer courses for those who have never placed gear, advanced technique, as well as day or multi-day guiding.

Courses included: Traditional Gear Placement, Leading Traditional Climbs, Traditional Climbing Practice, Traditional Gear Anchors

For Traveling Climbers

Discover the culture and astounding natural beauty of Taiwan. Explore Long Dong and other gems of the Isla Formosa's mountains, beaches, and towns with us. We can arrange anything from a quick day trip, to week long adventures. For the autonomous traveler, we offer gear rental (harnesses, draws rope, shoes, protection) and assistance with transportation information.

For a detailed listing of courses, please see the course catalog below.

Course Catalog

Introduction to Climbing (Artificial Wall)

Description: This is a first course in climbing. Students will learn basic climbing safety, such as how to identify general hazards in their chosen climbing environment, how to evaluate and mitigate risk, how to tie in with a figure 8 knot, and how to belay a top-roped climber. Basic movement technique over vertical terrain will also be demonstrated. Students will have the opportunity to complete 2 top-rope climbs. Since this course is presented in an artificial environment, students are encouraged to seek out additional instruction in order to climb in a natural environment, such as the courses offered below.

Course Topics: Risk Management. Tying in. Top-rope Belay. Movement Technique.

Duration: Approximately 2 hrs.

Location: Fuhe Bridge Tower or Nangang Sports Center

Enrollment: Maximum 3 people

Prerequisites: None

Movement Practice (Artificial Wall)

Description: Students will learn more advanced techniques for movement in a vertical environment such as how to negotiate different types of terrain from low-angle slab to steep overhangs and roofs, how to use different handholds such as sidepulls, underclings, crimps, slopers, pinches, pockets, how to use their feet well, i.e smearing, edging, stemming, matching, hooking and flagging. Students will then be presented with a variety of routes to challenge their movement ability, completing a minimum of two top-rope climbs or a set of 5 problems.

Course Topics: Negotiating various types of terrain. Advanced Foot Technique. Various Handholds.

Duration: 1 hr.

Location: Fuhe Bridge Tower, Nangang Sports Center or GongGuan Bouldering Wall

Enrollment: Maximum 3 people

Prerequisites: None

Lead Climbing (Artificial Wall)

Description: This course will help you discover what it takes to become a leader in climbing, take control of your own climbing experience, and grow to be an autonomous and responsible climber. Students will learn how to identify generals hazards of leading, how to assess risk while on lead, how to set up a stance for clipping, and proper clipping technique.Sometimes leaders must lead from behind as well, so the essentials of lead belaying will be covered. Students will then have the opportunity to lead 2 climbs and belay at least 1 lead climb to integrate all the skills necessary to be a good leader. Students completing this course or who have the equivalent experience are still strongly encouraged to take Lead Climbing (Natural Rock) before they attempt to lead outside.

Course Topics: Risk Assessment. Proper Quickdraw Usage. Lead Belay.

Duration: 2 hours

Location: Fuhe Bridge Tower or Nangang Sports Center

Enrollment: Maximum 3 people

Prerequisites: Introduction to Climbing or equivalent, Movement Practice

Introduction to Climbing (Natural Rock)

Description: This is a first course in climbing outside. Students will learn basic climbing safety, such as how to identify general hazards in their chosen climbing environment, how to evaluate and mitigate of risk, how to tie in with a figure 8 knot, and belaying essentials. Basic movement technique over vertical terrain will also be demonstrated. Students will have the opportunity to complete 2 top-rope climbs. Students that have completed the indoor course or have the equivalent experience may take a review version of this course, if it fits their needs and learning style.

Course Topics: Risk Management. Tying in. Top-rope Belay. Movement Technique.

Duration: Approximately 3 hrs.

Location: Long Dong

Enrollment: Minimum 3 people, Maximum 5 people.

Prerequisites: None

Movement Practice (Natural Rock)

Description: Students will learn more advanced techniques for movement in a vertical environment such as how to negotiate different types of terrain from low-angle slab to steep overhangs and roofs, how to use different handholds such as sidepulls, underclings, crimps, slopers, pinches, pockets, how to use their feet well, i.e smearing, edging, stemming, matching, hooking and flagging. Students will then be presented with a variety of routes to challenge their movement ability, completing a minimum of two top-rope climbs or a set of 5 problems. Students who have completed the indoor courses are still encouraged to engage in this course fully, as movement over rock is vastly different than movement in an artificial environment.

Course Topics: Negotiating various types of terrain. Advanced Foot Technique. Various Handholds.

Duration: Approximately 3 hrs.

Location: Long Dong

Enrollment: Minimum 3 people, Maximum 5 people.

Prerequisites: Introduction to Climbing (Natural Rock) or equivalent.

Simple Anchors and Rappelling

Description: What do you do after you finish a climb? This class teaches the basics of anchoring yourself safely, i.e. how to build and take apart simple anchors. Then you will learn how use the rope to exit off a climb safely and self-sufficiently.

Course Topics: Setting Toprope Anchors. Cleaning Toprope Anchors. Rappelling. Autoblock. Fireman's Belay.

Duration: 1 hour

Location: Long Dong

Enrollment: Minimum 3 people, Maximum 5 people.

Prerequisites: Introduction to Climbing (Natural Rock) or equivalent.

Lead Climbing (Natural Rock)

Description: This course will help you discover what it takes to become a leader in climbing, take control of your own climbing experience, and grow to be an autonomous and responsible climber. Students will learn how to identify generals hazards of leading, how to assess risk while on lead, how to set up a stance for clipping, and proper clipping technique. Sometimes leaders must lead from behind as well, so the essentials of lead belaying will be covered. Students will then have the opportunity to lead 2 climbs and belay at least 1 lead climb to integrate all the skills necessary to be a good leader.

Course Topics: Risk Assessment. Proper Quickdraw Usage. Lead Belay. Bolt Safety and Ethics. Simple Recovery and Retreating Techniques. 2 Lead Climbs.

Duration: 3 hrs.

Location: Long Dong

Enrollment: Maximum 4 people.

Prerequisites: Introduction to Climbing (Natural Rock) or equivalent, Simple Anchors and Rappelling, Movement Practice (Natural Rock)

Lead Climbing Practice (Natural Rock)

Description: This practice session fosters greater comfort and confidence while leading on natural rock. It includes more movement practice. Students have the opportunity to apply the techniques for safe clipping, retreating, and cleaning in a minimum of 2 lead climbs.

Duration: 3 hours

Location: Long Dong

Enrollment: Maximum 3 people.

Prerequisites: Lead Climbing (natural rock) or equivalent

Traditional Gear Placement

Description: Placing traditional gear is a fine craft. This course imparts the fundamentals from first principles in physics. Students will practice in a thorough and intensive hands-on session on the ground. Then students will then have the opportunity to follow and observe the placements on two traditional climbs.

Course Topics: Fundamentals of passive gear placement. Fundamentals of active gear placement. Extending Gear. Judging rock quality.

Duration: 2 hrs.

Location: Long Dong

Enrollment: Maximum 4 people.

Prerequisites: None

Leading Traditional Climbs

Description: So now you've honed your placements into a natural skill, and you're ready to step up and become a real leader. This course will take you through that process. You will have the opportunity to lead through a progression of at least 3 climbs: 1. Practice lead with toprope backup. 2. Practice lead with fixed gear backup. 3. Practice lead with no backup.

Course Topics: Appropriate Stances for placement. Risk assessment. Fall forces on gear.

Duration: 5 hrs.

Location: Long Dong

Enrollment: Maximum 2 people.

Prerequisites: Traditional Gear Placement, Lead Climbing (Natural Rock) or equivalent, Movement Practice (Natural Rock) strongly recommended.

Traditional Climbing Practice

Description: A full day of traditional lead climbing practice.

Duration: 7 hrs.

Location: Long Dong

Enrollment: Maximum 4 people.

Prerequisites: Leading Traditional Climbs

Traditional Gear Anchors

Description: This course is designed to teach you all the skills necessary to build your own anchors out of traditional gear. It is for people interested in completing multi-pitch climbs or who would like to climb in back-country or unexplored areas.

Course Topics: Various use cases and choosing appropriate anchor setups. Assessing fall forces and direction of fall. Building solid anchors according to SARENE principles. Various anchor ties. Rappelling from gear anchors.

Duration: 3 hours.

Location: Long Dong

Enrollment: Maximum 5 people.

Prerequisites: Simple Anchors and Rappelling, Traditional Gear Placement

Advanced Nutcraft

Description: Sometimes the features in the rock do not conform to textbook placements. This practical course teaches you skills for those situations.

Course Topics: Offset nut placements. Opposing nuts and Horizontal nut placements. Stacking nut placements. Tricam and Hex placements.

Duration: 2 hours.

Location: Long Dong

Enrollment: Maximum 3 people.

Prerequisites: Traditional Gear Placement

Multi-pitch Climbing

Description: Get ready for longer adventures. This course teaches the basics of climbing multi-pitch climbs, and is your platform to learn the skills necessary to climb tall routes at international destinations.

Course Topics: Building and judging integrity of belay stations. Swinging and swapping leads. Rope Management. 2 multi-pitch climbs.

Duration: 5 hours.

Location: Long Dong

Enrollment: Maximum 2 people.

Prerequisites: Leading Traditional Climbs, Traditional Gear Anchors
